Recipe

Ingredientes

  • 4 cups red or pinto beans
  • 7 cups water in which the beans had boiled in
  • 1 can (13.5 ounces) coconut milk
  • 2 cans (24 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 pound batata, cubed
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, peeled and cut in one piece
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 10 whole cloves
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 2 packs (6 ounces) small milk round crackers

Preparación - Parte 1

  1. Soak dry beans in water overnight. The next day discard water in beans. Boil the beans in a large pot with plenty of water, adding water as needed, for about 2 hours, or until tender.
  2. Blend together beans and water for about 30 seconds, or until smooth. If necessary, add more water. Pass the mixture through a strainer into a large bowl and set aside.
  3. Combine the beans, the coconut milk, and the evaporated milk in a large pot over medium heat.
  4. When it breaks a boil, stir in the salt, sugar, sweet potatoes, ginger, vanilla, cinnamon sticks, clove, and raisins.

Preparación - Parte 2

  1. Let simmer, stirring occasionally, for about 45-50 minutes, or until the liquid reduces to desired thickness and the sweet potato is tender. Remove from the heat and let cool.
  2. Serve warm or cold, add crackers when ready to eat. If eating cold, place it in the refrigerator after cooling until it is cold.
  3. Sweet beans is traditionally served with small round milk crackers or toasted casabe (cassava bread) for added crunch.
  4. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Keep in mind that it thickens as it cools!
